REMEMBRANCE DAY

The first remenbrance day, also named The first Armistice Day commemoration, was dedicated by the British Empire in 1919. We remember Remembrance Day on November 11 in remembrance of the First World War, which came to an end on that day in 1918 at 11 a.m., marking the eleventh hour of the eleventh day of the eleventh month, therefore we marked Remembrance Day on that date. On November 11 of each year, Canadians observe a moment of silence in honor of the men and women who have served and are still serving our nation in times of war, crisis, and peace.
